# Governance Response Memo (Spokeo, Inc. v. Robins)

> Use this to respond to an audit or regulatory request focused on governance: roles, committees, reporting, escalation, and accountability.

---

## Purpose

This memo provides a formal governance response to oversight, audit, or regulatory questions triggered by oversight questions about data-accuracy governance after Spokeo and related FCRA exposure. It explains governance design, escalation pathways, accountability, and board-level reporting so reviewers can evaluate whether leadership oversight is effective and durable.

<p><strong>Context: </strong>This memo responds to examiner and oversight requests regarding oversight questions about data-accuracy governance after Spokeo and related FCRA exposure. It summarizes governance arrangements after the Supreme Court decision at 578 U.S. 330 and ongoing accuracy/dispute governance requirements and explains how accountability and board-level reporting were strengthened for durable oversight.</p>

<p><strong>Governance Model: </strong>Governance committees receive periodic reports on dispute-volume trends, correction SLAs, source-lineage controls, and recurring audit findings. Reporting structures and decision records are maintained for internal and external review.</p>

<p><strong>Security Ownership: </strong>The CISO governs security controls for data integrity systems while data-governance and legal functions co-own accuracy procedures and dispute escalation. Material exceptions and unresolved quality issues are escalated with documented accountability.</p>

<p><strong>Risk and Control Oversight: </strong>Accuracy-control exceptions, escalation decisions, and policy updates are tracked with owners and due dates. Governance artifacts include committee minutes, KPI dashboards, and remediation registers supporting legal and oversight responses.</p>
