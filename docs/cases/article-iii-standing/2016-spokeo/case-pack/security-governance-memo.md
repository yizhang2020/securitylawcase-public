# Security Governance Memo (Spokeo, Inc. v. Robins)

> Use this to define or clarify security governance: roles, committees, escalation paths, and accountability; ensures “who decides what” is clear.

---

## Purpose

This memo clarifies governance roles, escalation triggers, and reporting responsibilities needed to manage risks surfaced by Spokeo, Inc. v. Robins. It ensures that leadership, legal, and security functions operate under a common accountability model.

## Hallucinated writing examples

**Scenario:** In an illustrative period following the Supreme Court ruling on Article III standing in Spokeo **(time)**, the Chief Information Security Officer **(role)** prepares a security governance memo **(type)** for Executive Leadership, Security Leadership, Privacy and Legal Stakeholders **(audience)**.

<div class="writing-example-formal">

<p><strong>SECURITY GOVERNANCE MEMO</strong></p>

<div class="doc-meta">
<p><strong>To: </strong>Executive Leadership, Security Leadership, Privacy and Legal Stakeholders<br>
<strong>From: </strong>Chief Information Security Officer<br>
<strong>Date: </strong>January 16, 2017<br>
<strong>Subject: </strong>Security Governance — Data Accuracy Oversight and Escalation Accountability</p>
</div>

<p><strong>Purpose: </strong>This memo defines governance responsibilities for data-accuracy risk, dispute handling escalation, and security-control oversight in light of Spokeo-related standing and FCRA exposure concerns. It aligns operational accountability across security, privacy, legal, and data-governance functions.</p>

<p><strong>Governance Model: </strong>Governance bodies receive regular reporting on dispute aging, data-quality findings, exception trends, and remediation milestones. Reporting cadence and ownership models are documented to support oversight and legal defensibility.</p>

<p><strong>Roles and Escalation: </strong>The CISO owns security governance standards for systems handling sensitive profile data; privacy/legal leaders co-own escalation requirements for accuracy-related incidents. Material exceptions and unresolved risks escalate through executive governance with documented review dates and closure expectations.</p>

</div>
