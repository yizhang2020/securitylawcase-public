## Purpose

<p>Equifax continues to implement security and governance improvements following the 2017 cybersecurity incident and subsequent regulatory and legal actions. The Company remains committed to protecting consumer information and providing transparent updates on remediation and support resources.</p>

<p>The 2017 incident involved unauthorized access to systems containing consumer credit-file information. The Company has taken remediation actions, supported law enforcement and regulators, and implemented enhanced security controls across critical systems.</p>

<p>Impacted data categories associated with the 2017 event included personal information used in credit-related services. Equifax continues to provide resources for affected individuals and supports settlement and redress mechanisms as required.</p>

<p>Equifax has strengthened vulnerability management, privileged-access controls, monitoring, and governance reporting. Independent assessments and oversight processes are in place to track remediation and control effectiveness.</p>

<p>Consumers should monitor account and credit activity and use available identity-protection resources. For official updates and support options, visit [URL] or contact Equifax support via designated channels.</p>
