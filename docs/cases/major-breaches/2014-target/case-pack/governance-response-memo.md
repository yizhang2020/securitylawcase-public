# Governance Response Memo (In re Target Corp. MDL)

> Use this to respond to an audit or regulatory request focused on governance: roles, committees, reporting, escalation, and accountability.

---

## Purpose

This memo provides a formal governance response to oversight, audit, or regulatory questions triggered by Target MDL oversight and remediation governance after the 2013 payment-card incident. It explains governance design, escalation pathways, accountability, and board-level reporting so reviewers can evaluate whether leadership oversight is effective and durable.

<p><strong>Context: </strong>This memo responds to examiner and oversight requests regarding Target MDL oversight and remediation governance after the 2013 payment-card incident. It summarizes governance arrangements after public MDL litigation and associated discovery obligations requiring durable governance evidence and explains how accountability and board-level reporting were strengthened for durable oversight.</p>

<p><strong>Governance Model: </strong>Board and executive committees receive recurring updates on segmentation progress, vendor access governance, and forensic evidence readiness. Committee materials and escalation logs are retained for audit and litigation support.</p>

<p><strong>Security Ownership: </strong>The CISO owns policy and standards for payment-environment security and coordinates with legal, internal audit, and operations on remediation governance. Exception approvals require compensating controls and defined review dates.</p>

<p><strong>Risk and Control Oversight: </strong>Material findings and timeline variances are tracked through a formal risk register with owner accountability and closure criteria. Policy updates and exception decisions are documented and reviewed on cadence. Attachments include remediation governance charter, latest KPI pack, and exception tracker.</p>
