## Purpose

This document prepares a formal external statement framework for Yahoo (2018), balancing accuracy, legal sensitivity, and audience trust. It helps communications, legal, and security teams present consistent facts, remediation posture, and next steps without overstatement or omission.

<p>Yahoo confirms it is continuing to respond to previously disclosed unauthorized access incidents affecting user account data and to related legal and regulatory processes. The Company is implementing additional safeguards and will continue to provide updates through official channels as material facts are confirmed.</p>

<p>Yahoo previously disclosed large-scale compromise of user account data. The Company has preserved relevant evidence, engaged internal and external experts, and continues to coordinate with law enforcement and legal counsel. Public disclosures are limited to validated information to avoid confusion and protect investigative integrity.</p>

<p>The incidents involve user-account information. As investigations and legal proceedings continue, the Company is committed to accurate, timely updates regarding affected categories and mitigation options made available to impacted users.</p>

<p>Yahoo is strengthening account-security controls, improving monitoring and evidence retention, and maintaining governance reporting on remediation progress. The Company is also reviewing legacy systems and escalation workflows to reduce recurrence risk.</p>

<p>Users are encouraged to monitor account activity, update passwords, and enable available account-protection features. Please rely on official Yahoo support pages for updates and avoid phishing messages that reference this incident. Additional information is available at [URL].</p>
