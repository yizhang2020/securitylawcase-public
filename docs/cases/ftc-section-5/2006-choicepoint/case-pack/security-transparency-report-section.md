# Security Transparency Report Section (FTC v. ChoicePoint (2006))

> Use this to draft a section for an annual or ad-hoc transparency report covering security: requests received, incidents, and program highlights; supports accountability and stakeholder trust.

---

## Purpose

This section provides a structured transparency narrative for FTC v. ChoicePoint (2006), summarizing incident and governance context, program improvements, and measurable control progress for external stakeholders. It is designed for consistent recurring reporting.

## Hallucinated writing examples

**Scenario:** In an illustrative period following FTC findings on fraudulent account onboarding and consumer-data misuse **(time)**, the Security Director **(role)** prepares a security transparency report section **(type)** for leadership stakeholders **(audience)**.

<p><strong>SECURITY — TRANSPARENCY REPORT SECTION (DRAFT)</strong></p>

<div class="doc-meta">
<p><strong>Reporting period: </strong>January 1, 2006 – December 31, 2006<br>
<strong>Scope: </strong>Subscriber onboarding integrity, data access, and fraud prevention controls<br>
<strong>Prepared for: </strong>Annual Trust / Transparency Report<br>
<strong>Date: </strong>March 2007</p>
</div>

<p><strong>Overview: </strong>This section summarizes security and governance measures implemented to protect consumer data and to address weaknesses identified in the FTC enforcement context.</p>

<p><strong>Material Cybersecurity Incident: </strong>Unauthorized parties obtained consumer data by posing as legitimate business customers and exploiting weaknesses in account onboarding controls. During this period, incident response and remediation focused on preventing recurrence through stricter onboarding and monitoring.</p>

<p><strong>Regulatory and Legal Outcomes: </strong>The FTC settlement required strengthened information-security practices, oversight, and independent assessments. We aligned governance and operational controls to these obligations and maintained records to support examination and accountability.</p>

<p><strong>Program Highlights (2006): </strong>Program highlights include stronger business-customer verification, fraud analytics expansion, improved access monitoring, and clearer escalation pathways for suspicious onboarding activity. Progress is reviewed through recurring control and compliance reporting. References: [FTC matter], [Public notice]. For questions: [contact].</p>
