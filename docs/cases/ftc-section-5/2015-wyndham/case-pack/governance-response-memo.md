# Governance Response Memo (FTC v. Wyndham Worldwide Corp.)

> Use this to respond to an audit or regulatory request focused on governance: roles, committees, reporting, escalation, and accountability.

---

## Purpose

This memo provides a formal governance response to oversight, audit, or regulatory questions triggered by Wyndham governance questions tied to the stipulated injunction and franchise connectivity risks. It explains governance design, escalation pathways, accountability, and board-level reporting so reviewers can evaluate whether leadership oversight is effective and durable.

<p><strong>Context: </strong>This memo responds to examiner and oversight requests regarding Wyndham governance questions tied to the stipulated injunction and franchise connectivity risks. It summarizes governance arrangements after the Third Circuit decision (799 F.3d 236) and the stipulated order entered December 11, 2015 and explains how accountability and board-level reporting were strengthened for durable oversight.</p>

<p><strong>Governance Model: </strong>Board oversight includes periodic reporting on franchise conformance, connectivity exceptions, and assessor findings. Governance committees track order milestones and require escalation for delayed remediation.</p>

<p><strong>Security Ownership: </strong>The CISO is accountable for policy, standards, and control oversight across property and corporate environments, with explicit authority to escalate material risk acceptance decisions. Legal and audit functions review governance artifacts supporting order compliance.</p>

<p><strong>Risk and Control Oversight: </strong>Control exceptions and assessment findings are managed through a formal escalation and closure process with defined owners and deadlines. Policy approvals, committee briefings, and exception decisions are retained as evidence for regulator and audit review.</p>
