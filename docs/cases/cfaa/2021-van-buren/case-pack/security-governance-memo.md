# Security Governance Memo (Van Buren v. United States (2021))

> Use this to define or clarify security governance: roles, committees, escalation paths, and accountability; ensures “who decides what” is clear.

---

## Purpose

This memo clarifies governance roles, escalation triggers, and reporting responsibilities needed to manage risks surfaced by Van Buren v. United States (2021). It ensures that leadership, legal, and security functions operate under a common accountability model.

## Hallucinated writing examples

**Scenario:** In an illustrative period following the Supreme Court Van Buren interpretation of CFAA authorized access **(time)**, the Chief Information Security Officer **(role)** prepares a security governance memo **(type)** for Executive Leadership, Security Leadership, Legal and HR Stakeholders **(audience)**.

<p><strong>SECURITY GOVERNANCE MEMO</strong></p>

<div class="doc-meta">
<p><strong>To: </strong>Executive Leadership, Security Leadership, Legal and HR Stakeholders<br>
<strong>From: </strong>Chief Information Security Officer<br>
<strong>Date: </strong>October 12, 2021<br>
<strong>Subject: </strong>Security Governance — Authorized-Access Oversight and Insider-Risk Escalation</p>
</div>

<p><strong>Purpose: </strong>This memo defines governance roles and escalation mechanics for insider misuse and authorized-access risk after Van Buren narrowed certain CFAA theories. It clarifies how security, legal, and HR functions coordinate decision-making and exception governance.</p>

<p><strong>Governance Model: </strong>Executive risk forums receive recurring updates on privileged-access reviews, query-monitoring findings, and insider-risk response metrics. Governance structures, escalation thresholds, and reporting cadence are documented for audit and legal review.</p>

<p><strong>Roles and Escalation: </strong>The CISO owns governance standards for access controls and monitoring. Legal and HR co-own misuse escalation pathways and response governance. Material policy exceptions and unresolved high-risk findings escalate to executive governance with mitigation plans and revisit dates.</p>
